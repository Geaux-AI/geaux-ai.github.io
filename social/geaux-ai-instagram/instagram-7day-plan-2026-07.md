This is automation at its simplest: taking a thing you do repeatedly and letting a tool do it instead.

Start here. Master this. Then we get weird.

## DAY 3: WEDNESDAY — Education + Analytical

**Pillar:** Education  
**Format:** "Why It Fails" Breakdown  
**Engagement Hook:** Contrary to expectation (makes people save/share)

### Hook
"Most automation projects fail within 3 months. We watched it happen 50 times. Here's the actual reason."

### Caption
People think automation fails because the tool isn't powerful enough.

Wrong. It fails because they automate before they map.

Here's what happens: a team picks a tool (Zapier, Make, whatever). Then they build in it. Then they realize their process is broken, so the automation is broken too. Then they stop using it.

The win teams don't talk about: they document their workflow first. They ask: who does this? When? Why? What are the edge cases? Only then do they automate.

Automation is just your process running without you. If your process is messy, automation makes it faster—and more broken.

Clean the path first. Then build the system.

## DAY 5: FRIDAY — Workflow/Behind-the-Scenes + Analytical

**Pillar:** Workflow  
**Format:** How We Actually Build Agents  
**Engagement Hook:** Transparency (builds trust; people are curious)

### Hook
"Here's what you don't see when we build an AI agent for a client. The messy middle part."

### Caption
You see the final result: an agent that reads invoices and files them. Looks simple. It's not.

Here's our actual process:

**Week 1:** Interview. Watch the process. Ask a hundred annoying questions.

**Week 2:** Build the rough version. We use real data from their system. It's messy. Invoices with typos, weird formats, dates that don't make sense.

**Week 3:** Watch it fail. A lot. Fix the failures. Add rules. Improve the logic.

**Week 4:** It just works. The client thinks we're geniuses.

The secret: we test with *their actual data*, not clean examples. We expect failure. We plan for edge cases.

Most tools and templates skip weeks 2 and 3. They build pretty demos. Real work is ugly.

That's why we do this.

## DAY 6: SATURDAY — Education + Contrarian

**Pillar:** Education  
**Format:** Rethink Common Advice  
**Engagement Hook:** Goes against the grain (high engagement, shares, saves)

### Hook
"Everyone says 'automate everything.' We're saying: automate the wrong thing and you'll regret it."

### Caption
The automation world is full of garbage advice.

"Automate everything!" "Build an agent for every task!" "Use AI to replace people!"

Here's the contrarian take: automate the thing that breaks your business first, not the thing that annoys you most.

A client could automate their social media posting (annoying, takes 30 mins a week) or automate their customer follow-up system (chaotic, loses them deals weekly). 

Which matters more? One gives you time. One gives you money.

We start with revenue-blocking problems. Then we move to time-saving ones. The order matters.

If you automate wrong, you've just built a faster broken system. The speedup doesn't matter.

Look at your business. What's costing you money right now? Not time. Money. Start there.
