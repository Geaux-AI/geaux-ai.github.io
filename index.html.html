What We Solve

Different missions, same admin chaos — six patterns we see across campus

Every engagement starts with the same question: what's the real problem, and what's the simplest way to solve it?

Academic Clubs

Event sign-ups that nobody tracks

RSVP lists live in email threads. Attendance gets marked manually (or not at all). No one knows who actually showed up to what.

Professional Societies

Dues collection is a nightmare

The treasurer tracks who paid in a spreadsheet. Reminders go out sporadically. Accounts don't reconcile, and transfers to national never balance.

Business Clubs

Point systems live in someone's head

Event attendance, volunteer hours, and committee work all feed into points — but the math is manual, inconsistent, and lost when officers graduate.

Honor Societies

Member eligibility tracking is tribal

Who meets the GPA? Who completed the service hours? Which seniors get awards? The answers live in one person's memory, not a system.

Social & Community Orgs

Communications fragment across platforms

Event details in GroupMe, meeting notes in Google Docs, rosters in email, announcements on Instagram. Nothing stays in sync.

Greek Life

Officer transitions lose everything

Every semester, crucial workflows and procedures disappear. The new treasurer has to reinvent dues tracking from scratch.

Our Approach

A four-phase pipeline where AI analyzes and humans decide

Six specialist agents study your problem in sequence — each one reads and challenges the analysis before it. Then people take over. Nothing gets built, sent, or changed without a human saying yes.

Phase 01

Discovery & Analysis

Six specialist agents — strategy, finance, events, records, membership, compliance — analyze your problem in a feedback chain and produce the tools, time savings, cost, and a compliance status.

Output: recommendation
Phase 02

Human Review

Our team validates the agents' work, then presents it to your board: here's what we found, what it costs, what it saves. You say yes, no, or "modify it" — and modify sends it back for re-analysis.

Gate: your decision
Phase 03

Build & QA

Only on a yes does our Developer agent build the automation. Every build then passes a Compliance review — LSU guidelines, national bylaws, data privacy — before anything reaches you.

Gate: compliance QA
Phase 04

Deliver & Iterate

We train your board and hand over a plain-English guide that survives officer turnover. Your feedback at 2, 4, and 8 weeks goes back through the same build-and-QA loop.

Loop: your feedback
Problem first. Agents analyze what's actually broken before recommending anything.
Only build when needed. If a $0 spreadsheet fix solves it, we say so — and the developer agent never runs.
Humans decide. Agents recommend; your board and our team make every call.
Zero lock-in. Built on tools you already own — Sheets, Forms, Zapier, Airtable, Notion — and documented so any future board can run it.
The Swarm

Nine specialists — one for every seat on your exec board

Agents draft, organize, and track. People approve. Nothing touches money, member data, or messages without a human saying so. When an agent recommends a fix, it names the exact apps to connect and walks your officers through wiring them up.

The President Agent

Executive Oversight

Keeps the whole org visible: meeting agendas, project status ledgers, deadline tracking, and delegation follow-ups — so nothing lives in one person's head.

The Vice President Agent

Internal Operations

Coordinates committees and builds officer transition documents, so every procedure survives graduation and the next board doesn't start from scratch.

The Treasurer Agent

Finances & Dues

Dues collection workflows, reminder messages ready for your treasurer to send, event budgets, and Venmo/QuickBooks/Google Sheets ledgers you can reconcile in minutes.

The Secretary Agent

Records & Minutes

Meeting minutes formatted and filed, rosters kept current, attendance logged automatically, and member correspondence templated and consistent.

The Events Chair Agent

Programming

RSVP tracking that actually works, day-of run sheets, venue and catering checklists, and post-event attendance synced straight to your points ledger.

The Marketing Chair Agent

PR & Communications

Announcement blasts across GroupMe, Instagram, and email from one draft. Content calendars, flyer copy, and a consistent voice for your org.

The Recruitment Chair Agent

Membership Growth

Interest form pipelines, automatic follow-ups with prospective members, rush event coordination, and onboarding sequences for new members.

The Service Chair Agent

Philanthropy & Service

Volunteer hour tracking per member, fundraiser ledgers that reconcile, and outreach templates for community partners and sponsors.

The Risk Manager Agent

Compliance & Eligibility

Helps your officers spot the right questions to ask — LSU guidelines, national bylaws, data privacy — and keeps member eligibility organized in one place. Decisions stay with your board.
