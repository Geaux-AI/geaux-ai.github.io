How It Works

We diagnose first. Then we recommend. We never lead with the tool.

Think of it like any good service call. You describe what's wrong, we look before we quote, and we only do the work that fixes it. The order matters. Problem first, solution second.

Step 01

You describe the problem

Plain language, no jargon. You don't need to know whether the fix is an agent or a spreadsheet. Just tell us what's slowing you down and what solved looks like.

Step 02

We determine and delegate

Before we propose anything, we scope the actual problem and route it to whichever specialist knows the domain best. Diagnosis first, no solution pitched yet.

Step 03

We find the right sized fix

Then we pick the fit and tell you honestly. Sometimes it's an agent or a skill. Sometimes a simple workflow or a connector. Sometimes the best answer isn't AI at all.

Step 04

We build only what fits

Proven end to end, reviewed with you until you say yes, then handed off as your own, with no lock in. If nothing needs building, we'll tell you that too.

When AI is the answer, we size it to the job. Most fixes land in one of these. We'll always tell you which:

Agent: a standalone Claude that owns a whole role.
Skill or workflow: a repeatable procedure or documented process.
Subagent: handles one slice of a bigger job.
MCP server: a custom tool for a system with no existing integration.
Connector: a hosted link to a tool you already use.
Something simpler: a template or a smarter spreadsheet. Not every problem needs AI.
